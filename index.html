FAQ

Common questions.

How is this different from a CI/CD tool?+

CI/CD runs your pipeline. DryDock sits above it and governs it, reading across planning, code, testing, and infrastructure, and telling you what it means. CI/CD tools aren't built to do that on their own, and DryDock doesn't replace them.

Does Drydock remove humans from the loop?+

The opposite. It makes human oversight actually implementable. Your policy defines where human judgment is required: AI-authored code, high-risk changes, production deploys. Agents clear routine work automatically; the few decisions that need a person arrive with evidence and a recommendation, and every approval is logged as an audit record. You stay in command without becoming the bottleneck.

Will this slow my team down?+

No. That's the point of the agent model. Policies are enforced automatically, so most releases pass without touching a human queue. Only flagged work needs a decision. Teams typically ship faster because risk is caught at the diff, not at the last gate before production.

Can I own an agent for something specific to my org?+

Yes. Custom agents are built from simple triggers, conditions, and actions, with no code required. Monitor client SLAs, regional compliance, budget thresholds, documentation coverage, or anything else unique to how your teams work.

How do we get started?+

Request early access above. Onboarding connects your existing tools (GitHub, Jira, CI/CD, Slack) and the agent crew starts observing immediately. Most teams see their first governed release within the first week.
